Plot Summary

The story follows Scout Finch, her older brother Jem, and their father Atticus Finch, a principled lawyer. As Scout and Jem grow up in Maycomb, they confront the harsh realities of racism, prejudice, and human complexity.

  • Boo Radley and Childhood Adventures:

    • Scout and Jem, along with their friend Dill, are fascinated by their reclusive neighbor, Boo Radley. They weave imaginative tales about him but later learn about kindness and courage through his quiet actions.

  • Tom Robinos Trial:

    • Atticus is appointed to defend Tom Robinson, a Black man falsely accused of raping a white woman,Mayella Ewell. Despite presenting compelling evidence of Tom's innocence, the racist jury convicts him. Tom later dies while trying to escape prison.

  • Climatic Resolution

    • After the trial, Mayella’s father, BOB EWELL, seeks revenge on Atticus and his family. He attacks Scout and Jem, but Boo Radley saves them, killing Bob in the process. Scout finally meets Boo, recognizing his humanity and shattering her childhood fears.


Major Character

  • Scout Finch: The young, inquisitive protagonist.
  • Atticus Finch:Scout’s father, a lawyer, and a paragon of justice.
  • Jem Finch:Scout’s older brother who matures throughout the story.
  • Bob Radelly:A reclusive neighbor who becomes a symbol of goodness.
  • Bob Ewell: The villain of the story, representing ignorance and hatred.
